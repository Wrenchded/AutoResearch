What is Brittleness?

At its core, brittleness is an AI's inability to generalize beyond its training data. Instead of learning underlying principles, it memorizes patterns. When faced with even minor, real-world variations‚Äîor "edge cases"‚Äîits performance can collapse dramatically. This section explores this fundamental vulnerability.

Overfitting vs. Generalization

An overfitted model is like a student who crams for a test. They memorize specific answers but can't solve new problems. A well-generalized model understands the concepts and can apply them anywhere.

The Problem of Data Imbalance

AI models are only as good as the data they're trained on. Datasets often over-represent ideal conditions, leaving the AI unprepared for the less common but critical scenarios.

This biased model will be an expert in sunny day driving but dangerously naive in snow.
